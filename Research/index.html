Research

My research explores the intricate processes involved in atmospheric chemistry and physics, particularly focusing on aerosol formation and oxidation processes under varying atmospheric conditions. Using a combination of laboratory experiments, environmental modeling, and field measurements, I delve into the dynamics of chemical and physical transformations within aerosols. This comprehensive approach not only enhances our understanding of aerosol behavior but also contributes to the broader field of atmospheric sciences by linking microscopic processes to macroscopic atmospheric phenomena. My goal is to provide insights that can inform both air quality management and climate modeling, ensuring that our strategies for addressing environmental and atmospheric challenges are grounded in robust scientific knowledge.
